Matilda Shades

by: Alex Zúñiga

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

My two friends and I have had a joke about a cow named Matilda. My friend and I made a video with the same cow with sunglasses as a joke and when I got the chance to make a game I knew it had to be of the very same cow. I also wanted to incorporate the sunglasses and so i decided to make the sunglasses fall while Matilda the cow attempted to catch them.

2. What I Learned

User testing resulted in my game being much improved. Getting a second opinion and having a fresh pair of eyes is really important because they can often catch mistakes that you have missed. For example, one suggestion I was given was to make the game harder as time progressed. I was able to do this by making sunglasses fall at a faster rate as time passed which made my game a lot more fun.

3. Architecture of My Game

I had to use 6 classes in total. Each had its own important purpose. For example, the GameMaker class was in charge of drawing or opening the different panels, the different states of the game: Start, Game and End. Other classes were in charge of managing the different objects. The Sunglasses class was in charge of the sunglasses objects and the Matilda class was in charge of the cow named Matilda. They are each their own class but they interact with each other and work together to make sure the game operates correctly. An example is that while the Matilda class is in charge of the cow (like its speed), the GameMaker class is where I added the code for how Matilda was going to move around the screen.

4. Technical Implementation

One aspect of my game that I am proud of is the cow I used. I found a picture of a cow that I thought would fit and then found the other items to put on the cow like the glasses and the backpack. I cut out the cow and the other items and then placed the backpack and sunglasses on the cow. I rotated the cow to the placement I needed and finally I found a picture of a bucket that I liked and added that too.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most difficult part was probably adding the background. This is because I didn't want the background not to match with my cow image. What I ended up doing was picking a simple green background and playing the cow on that background and then using that new image of the cow. I then used that same background for the actual background of my window for the game. This made it so that my cow blended with the actual background of the window.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

If I continued working on this game and could improve it I would probably add levels so that the user earned new accessories for the cow as they progressed. Or I would make it so that each level that the user completed they got a certain amount of coins and could buy more accessories for Matilda the cow with those coins. I think that would make my game even better and more interesting.
